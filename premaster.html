DAE pre-master

Since XXX, I started the pre-master programme at Design Academy Eindhoven (DAE). Through the course, I was able to learn DAE's tone and manner. In that short time, I was exposed to new, authentic, design approaches and experienced many materials and methodologies. Based on that experience I produced an installation art, making "my private garden". I expressed the exciting yet tense DAE pre-master experience as a garden, and thought about the meaning of this course to me.

My life in Europe began as a jobless couch potato. Since I immigrated to the Netherlands due to marriage, my career went sideways and I did not have anything I could call my own when I arrived. Then I started the pre-master programme, and it became my first official schedule. After coming to Europe, I built more conscious habits. I had to constantly make new choices in my daily life, such as picking my shampoo or go-for snack. To track down my choices, I collected most of the containers from daily products, like a hamster. Also, since I had more time now compared to when I was employed, I started to grow plants, filling up my time by caring for them: picking up the dead leaves and moving the pots according to the sun's motion.

All of these new habits were unconsciously a defense mechanism to calm me down in this new chaotic environment. A form of meditation as I went through the most stressful period of my life. Paradoxically, by presenting these habits in my Zen garden, I aim to melt away the tension.
